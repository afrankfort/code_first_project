Period FAQ

Plastics and Chemicals

  • In the UK, the average menstruator uses over 11,000 disposable menstrual products in their lifetime.

  • Tampons, pads and panty liners and their packaging generate more than 200,000 tonnes of waste per year, and they all contain plastic.

  • The average user throws away an astonishing 125 to 150kg of tampons, pads and applicators in their lifetime. Plastic waste ends up in landfill or, even worse, in seas, rivers and beaches.

  • Avoid lubricated and fragranced products; they are polluting, and generate unnecessary additional exposure to potentially synthetic chemicals.

  • 4 plastic bags worth of plastic on average in a pack of menstrual pads meaning it is made from 90% plastics

Environment

  • Don't flush your disposable menstrual products down the toilet; bag and bin them.

  • Even products labelled biodegradable or flushable shouldn't be flushed, as they may take months to break down, blocking pipes and polluting marine environment.

  • 1.5-2 billion menstrual items are flushed down Britain’s toilets each year, causing massive sewage and waterway issues.

  • It takes 500-800 yrs for disposable menstrual products and nappies to biodegrade.

Period Poverty

The Costs of Menstruating

  • A study by Plan International UK, from a survey of 1000 14 - 21 yr olds, found that 10% of girls could not afford menstrual products.

  • The study also found that 12% of girls had to improvise menstrual wear due to affordability issues and 14% had to ask to borrow menstrual products from a friend.

  • The average user spends £75 a year on menstrual products, and £18,450 in a lifetime on associated costs like pain relief, new underwear and chocolate.
